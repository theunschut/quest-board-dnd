<objective>
Tighten Email Stats to SuperAdmin-only and give the whole app a real Access Denied page. Two coupled changes: (1) add a method-level `[Authorize(Policy = "SuperAdminOnly")]` to `AdminController.EmailStats` so an Admin-but-not-SuperAdmin is rejected at the server, and (2) wire `ConfigureApplicationCookie`'s `AccessDeniedPath` to a real `/Account/AccessDenied` action reusing the already-existing (orphaned) `AccessDenied.cshtml` view, generalized off its Dungeon-Master-specific copy.

Purpose: ACCESS-01 requires server-side enforcement (a direct URL hit rejected, not just a hidden nav link). Today no `ConfigureApplicationCookie` exists, so every `AdminOnly`/`DungeonMasterOnly`/`SuperAdminOnly` failure across the app silently 404s — D-07 explicitly approved fixing this app-wide, not narrowly for Email Stats.
Output: EmailStats gated to SuperAdmin, a wired AccessDenied page, generalized view copy, and integration tests proving the Admin-vs-SuperAdmin distinction plus the AccessDenied action.

This plan is deliberately layout-free: the Email Stats *nav link* gating and the BoardType nav gating both live in `_Layout*.cshtml` and are done together in Plan 03 (Wave 2) to keep single-owner file semantics. This plan is the server-side authorization + page wiring that Plan 03's nav link points at.
</objective>
